Quantitative Strategy Discovery

Case Study 1: Capital Preservation for HOS Premium Payment

Investor Profile: Mr. Chan, a 60-year-old nearing retirement, has just received a lump sum from his MPF. He plans to use HK$500,000 to pay for the premium of a Home Ownership Scheme (HOS) flat in 3 years.

Strategy: Conservative (80% Fixed-Income / 20% Equity).

Analytical Finding: Mr. Chan already 60 years old, and he has to use this capital to pay for the flat in 3 years, which mean he cannot accept a high risk investment. The simulation calculate HK$546,363.5. For short-term liabilities in HK's inflationary environment, low-volatility debt instruments brings lower risk, ensuring the liquidity remains intact for the HOS transaction.

Case Study 2: Real Growth for Middle-Class Housing Down Payment

Investor Profile: Ms. Wong is a 28 years old healthcare professional with HK$200,000 in initial capital, aiming to accumulate a residential down payment over a 7-year horizon.

Strategy: Moderate (50% Equity / 50% Fixed-Income).

Analytical Finding: Ms. Wong is a worker who has salary to take some risk. Her portfolio has reached HK$300,726.05. This strategy helps your money grow faster than the cost of living. This is important in Hong Kong because housing prices usually go up very fast.
Reference: Census and Statistics Department, HKSAR (2024). Historical analysis of Hong Kong CCPI and residential property price indices.

Case Study 3: Life-Cycle Investing for Fresh Graduates

Investor Profile: A 22 years old CityU fresh graduate investing a HK$20,000 into a retirement-focused portfolio with a 35-year time horizon.

Strategy: Aggressive (90% Equity / 10% Fixed-Income).

Analytical Finding: The student is a fresh graduate. He has enough risk tolerance because he is young. Retirement focus plan is low risk, and his invest amount is small. The final value is more than HK$560,000. Therefore, young people should use a high-equity strategy early in their careers.
Reference: Bodie, Z., Merton, R. C., & Samuelson, W. F. (1992). Labor supply flexibility and portfolio choice in a life cycle model.

Future Works

While this simulation give a basic knowledge of financial planning, future works will fix some current limitations:

  • Automatic Investment Adjustment: Make the system change to using 'Glide Path' to automatically reduce risk when you are closer to your target date. This keeps your portfolio more stable.

  • Simulating More Market Scenarios: Use Monte Carlo simulation to predict 10,000+ market paths, showing the probability of all the possible results instead of just one case.

  • Calculating Extra Costs: Calculate Hong Kong's specific costs like stamp duty, brokerage fees and ETF management fees, to provide a more realistic amount of the actual returns.
