Investment Philosophy & Analysis

Time Horizon (Q1)

How long you plan to invest will affect how much risk you can take. If you have a longer time, you can afford to wait for the market to recover if it goes down. (Fisher & Statman, 1997)

Goal Setting (Q2)

Your investment plan should be based on your specific life goals, like buying a house or retirement, instead of just looking at general market risks. (Brunel, 2003)

Loss Aversion (Q3)

Most people feel the pain of losing money much more than the joy of making it. This often makes investors panic and sell at the wrong time. (Kahneman & Tversky, 1979)

Asset Allocation (Q4)

The strategic selection between risky and risk-free assets is the primary driver of portfolio performance. (Brinson et al., 1986)

Risk Capacity (Q5)

Risk capacity is defined by the objective ability to sustain a loss without compromising a standard of living. (Grable, 2000)

Life Stage (Q6)

As you get older, your investment style should change. Younger people can usually take more risks, while those closer to retirement should focus on stability. (Bodie et al., 1992)
